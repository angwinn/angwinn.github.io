Nutella Thumbprint Cookies1.21.2019

There’s just something about Nutella. I’m not sure if it’s just all nostalgic because I’ve had Nutella since I was a toddler, or if simply because it’s chocolate! Nonetheless, I made these cookies on a whim because Papou (which is grandpa in Greek) keeps bringing Nutella over to our house. One time we had 5 unopened jars. The love for it clearly runs deep and obviously the apple doesn’t fall far from the tree.

Without further ado here’s the most decadent Nutella cookies ever!

Ingredients:

1 large egg room temperature

3/4 cup Nutella room temperature

1 cup all-purpose-flour spoon and level

For the filling:

1/2 cup Nutella room temperature

Crushed nuts (optional)

Instructions:

- Mix the egg and Nutella until it’s well incorporated.

- Slowly add the flour (you may not need all of it, just enough to where it’s not sticky to the touch)

- Wrap the bowl and let it sit in the fridge for about 30 minutes.

Directions:

- Roll them into small balls and make a little dent in the top for the filling. It may be easier to pipe the filling instead of using a spoon.

- Bake at 350 degrees for 10 minutes!

- Sprinkle with any kind of crushed nuts!

Let me know if you try these out in the comments!! They’ve definitely become a staple in our home when we have company since they are so easy to make!!
